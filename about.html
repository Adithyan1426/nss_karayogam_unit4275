About Nair Service Society

The Nair Service Society, a synonym for selfless service and social commitment, was founded by the late Sri. Mannathu Padmanabhan on 31st October 1914. It came into being at a crucial stage in the history of the Nair community, which was facing a crisis brought about by disunity and blind adherence to outmoded customs, beliefs, and practices.

They had a glorious past, but ignorance accumulated over centuries—reinforced by inadequate education—proved disastrous. Their fondness for celebrations and extravaganza led to a decline. Loss of land holdings, underemployment, and lack of modern education worsened the situation.

At this juncture, Sri. Mannathu Padmanabhan united the community and founded the N.S.S. Through tireless efforts and vision, the N.S.S established numerous educational, healthcare, and social institutions across Kerala.

With a strong grassroots network of Karayogams and Taluk Unions, the N.S.S remains a well-organized body committed to its noble principles. It now runs over a hundred schools, 15 arts and science colleges, 3 training colleges, 1 engineering college, 1 homeo medical college, several nursing colleges, technical institutes, and working women hostels.

While its primary aim was to uplift the Nair community, the N.S.S today serves all sections of society and plays a vital role in the social, educational, and economic development of Kerala.
