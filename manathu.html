Mannathu Padmanabhan

Early Life

Mannathu Padmanabhan was born on January 2, 1878, in Perunna village, Changanassery, Kerala. He was the son of Easwaran Namboothiri and Mannathu Parvathy Amma. He started his career as a teacher in 1893 and later became a lawyer in 1905.

Founder of Nair Service Society (NSS)

On 31st October 1914, he founded the Nair Service Society (NSS) to unite and uplift the Nair community through education and social reform. Under his leadership, NSS established several schools, colleges, and hostels across Kerala.

Freedom Fighter & Social Reformer

Mannathu Padmanabhan actively participated in the Indian freedom struggle. He took part in the Vaikom Satyagraha and the Temple Entry Proclamation movement. He also promoted communal harmony and modern education in Kerala.

Legacy & Awards

In 1959, he was awarded the Padma Bhushan by the Government of India. In 1966, he received the title "Bharata Kesari" from the President of India. His birthday, January 2, is celebrated as "Mannam Jayanti" in Kerala.

Death

Mannathu Padmanabhan passed away on February 25, 1970, but his legacy lives on through the institutions he founded and the lives he changed.
